Reflection Blog

17 April 2018

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

An immersive high intensity course that focuses as much on personal development as teaching technical skills. I see it as a disciplined yet caring environment that encourages students to build strong inter-personal relationships and problem solving skills.

What are your impressions?

Seems like another world to me, I am beginning to understand the ‘mindset’ or attitudes and ideologies of this program and the behaviours favoured in careers in the sector.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

It will challenge me but ultimately I think I will find it very rewarding. I am interested to see how I engage in group environments as I have little experience in this kind of space. I think it will favour inquisitive outlook and pay off to learn creatively and accept that failure is a part of this.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

The video reinforced my expectations, I am coming to terms with the intensity of the process!

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

I am excited to learn to problem solve in pairs and larger groups and to get to know fellow students personally. Nervous to see if I can dig myself out of a difficult problem with grace and not by having a meltdown.
